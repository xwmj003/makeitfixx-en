P.A.: Fixative

1. Film-forming polymers: Such as PVP (polyvinylpyrrolidone)
or acrylic copolymer, which form a film
invisible on the skin to set makeup.

2. Moisturizing agents: Glycerin, hyaluronic acid and
Panthenol are used to keep the skin hydrated, avoiding
make your makeup crack or look dry.

3. Alcohols: Some fixatives contain alcohol so that
The product dries quickly on the skin, although they may
be irritating to sensitive skin.

4. Plant extracts: Aloe vera, chamomile or tea extracts
green, which soothe the skin and provide antioxidant benefits.



Collagen sheets:

1. Hydrolyzed Collagen: This is the form of collagen that is commonly used
in these leaves. It is fragmented into small molecules that
can be more easily absorbed through the skin.

2. Moisturizing Agents: Ingredients glycerin help attract and
retain moisture, which facilitates the dissolution of the
leaves in contact with wet skin.

3. Water Soluble Polymers: Such as carboxymethylcellulose or rubber.
xanthan gum, which helps maintain the structure of the leaf and
they break down upon contact with the
water or skin fluids.

4. Heat and Humidity: The skin's own heat and the moisture generated
during use (from sweat or applied serum) help the
sheets dissolve gradually.
